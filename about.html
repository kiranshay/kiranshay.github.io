About Me

Neuroscience meets Machine Learning

I'm a junior at Johns Hopkins University studying Computer Science and Neuroscience, with minors in Applied Mathematics & Statistics and Business.

My Mission

I'm driven by a simple question: how can we use computational tools to decode the complexity of living systems? My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and biological systems, using data-driven approaches to understand the brain and improve healthcare.

Current Research

I'm currently building TremorML, an ML pipeline that differentiates neurological tremor types from wearable sensor data. This is exactly the kind of applied ML for healthcare I want to pursue: taking complex biological signals and turning them into actionable clinical insights.

Recent Achievements

2025 JHU Design Day Digital Vanguard Award

Won for my Baseball Lineup Optimizer, which uses Markov chains and Monte Carlo simulation to maximize expected runs.

Beyond Research

I contributed to Delineo, JHU's infectious disease simulation engine, helping model stochastic disease spread across communities.

I also co-founded Affordable Tutoring Solutions as CTO, building EdTech infrastructure to serve students from underserved communities.
