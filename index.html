About Me

I'm a junior at Johns Hopkins University studying Computer Science and Neuroscience, with minors in Applied Mathematics & Statistics and Business. I'm driven by a simple question: how can we use computational tools to decode the complexity of living systems?

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and biological systems. I'm currently building TremorML, an ML pipeline that differentiates neurological tremor types from wearable sensor data—exactly the kind of applied ML for healthcare I want to pursue.

I love stochastic modeling and optimization. My baseball lineup optimizer uses Markov chains and Monte Carlo simulation to maximize expected runs, and won the 2025 JHU Design Day Digital Vanguard Award.

Beyond my own research, I contribute to Delineo, JHU's infectious disease simulation engine, and co-founded Affordable Tutoring Solutions as CTO, building EdTech infrastructure to serve underserved communities.

Currently seeking research opportunities and collaborations in applied ML for biological systems. Let's build something meaningful together.
